The first time I read *Legends & Lattes* I was on a train that had been delayed
for three hours. I was tired. The weather was bad. The man next to me kept
sneezing.

By the time Viv rang the bell of that first coffee shop, I wasn't on the train
anymore. I was somewhere warm. Somewhere that smelled of cinnamon and old wood.
Somewhere a kind person was making something kind for someone else, and nothing
catastrophic was going to happen for a few hundred pages.

That's the thing cozy fantasy does that no other genre does as cleanly. It
gives you a warm room at the end of a long day, and then it respects you
enough not to burn the room down for drama.

*The Crossroads Inn* — the series that starts soon with Book 1, *The Keeper's
Reluctance* — is my attempt at that kind of room. The main character, Briar
Thornheart, is a retired mercenary who does not particularly want to be
retired, and does not at all want to be an innkeeper. She inherits the
Crossroads Inn by accident. She gets better at tending the fire than she
expects. She makes friends she didn't mean to make. That's roughly the
arc — over six books, slow as a season turning.

If that sounds like a room you'd like to sit in, I'll meet you there.

— Jason
